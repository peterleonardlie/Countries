Human Development Index World Happiness Report Data Sources

Data Sources


Country statistics were sourced from WorldBank.
The fields used are:
  • Population: a count of all residents of the country
  • Surface area: the country’s total area, in square kilometers
  • Population density: The average number of people per sq. km of land area
  • Life expectancy: the average number of years residents of the country live to
  • GDP: gross domestic product, which is the value of all goods and services produced in the country in a year, in US dollars
  • GDP growth: the annual growth rate of the country’s GDP as a percentage
  • Poverty: the percentage of the population living on less than $1.90 a day
  • Literacy: the percentage of people ages 15 and above who can read and write
  • Birth rate: the number of live births occurring during the year, per 1,000 people
  • Death rate: the number of deaths occurring during the year, per 1,000 people
  • CO2: the number in metric tons of carbon dioxide emitted per person

World Happiness values were sourced from Kaggle.
The fields used are:
  • Score: a score of the country’s happiness assessed from a survey of residents
  • Rank: the country’s happiness ranking globally
data is based on 2017's result.

Human Development Index values were sourced from the United Nations .
The fields used are:
  • Score: a score of the country’s level of human development as assessed by the UN
  • Rank: the country’s HDI ranking globally
data is based on 2014's result.

Similar Countries are determined using a K-Nearest Neighbour algorithm. The distance metric for this algorithm is Euclidean Distance. The algorithm takes into account Country Statistics, World Happiness and Human Development Index to find the 3 most similar countries.

The radar chart is created using Chart.js and on each line shows the country's percentile in the world for the area. A further out point means a higher percentile, meaning the country is performing better in that area.
